# What Is DSO (Days Sales Outstanding) — and How to Actually Reduce It

**Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)** measures the average number of days it takes to
collect cash after a sale. It's the single clearest signal of how efficiently your
business turns invoices into money in the bank. A rising DSO quietly starves your
cash flow even when revenue looks healthy.

## The DSO formula

```
DSO = (Accounts Receivable ÷ Total Credit Sales) × Number of Days
```

For a month:

> A company with $300,000 in receivables and $500,000 of credit sales over 30
> days has a DSO of **(300,000 ÷ 500,000) × 30 = 18 days**.

Use the same period length consistently (monthly, quarterly, annual) so the trend
is comparable.

## What counts as a "good" DSO?

There's no universal number — it depends on your payment terms and industry. A
useful rule of thumb: **DSO should be no more than ~1.3× your standard terms.** If
you invoice Net 30, a DSO above ~40 days means a meaningful share of customers are
paying late. See [our DSO benchmarks by industry](/benchmarks) for ranges.

## Why DSO creep is dangerous

- **It hides in plain sight.** Revenue and profit can look fine while cash dries up.
- **It compounds.** Every extra day of DSO is working capital you've effectively
  lent to customers, interest-free.
- **It's a leading indicator of bad debt.** The longer an invoice ages, the lower
  the odds it's ever paid in full.

## Seven proven ways to reduce DSO

1. **Invoice immediately and accurately.** The clock starts when the invoice
   lands. Same-day, error-free invoices remove the most common stall — disputes
   over wrong amounts or missing POs.
2. **Make terms explicit and short where you can.** "Net 30" beats "due on
   receipt" for clarity; shorter terms pull cash forward if your market tolerates
   it.
3. **Send reminders *before* and after the due date.** A polite nudge a few days
   before due dramatically lifts on-time payment. Most late payments are
   forgetfulness, not refusal.
4. **Use the right channel at the right time.** Email for early reminders, a
   firmer SMS as invoices age. Respect quiet hours — pressure at the wrong moment
   damages the relationship without speeding payment.
5. **Offer frictionless payment.** A one-click pay link in every reminder removes
   the "I'll do it later" gap.
6. **Prioritize by risk, not by age alone.** Not every overdue invoice deserves
   the same effort. Score which accounts are likely to slip and focus there first.
7. **Offer payment plans before accounts go to collections.** A structured plan
   recovers more than an ultimatum — and keeps the customer.
