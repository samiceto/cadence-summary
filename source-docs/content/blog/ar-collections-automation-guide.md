# Accounts Receivable Automation: A Practical Guide

"Accounts receivable automation" gets used loosely. This guide is concrete: what's
genuinely worth automating in collections, what you should keep a human in the
loop for, and how to roll it out without torching customer relationships.

## What AR automation actually does

A capable AR automation system handles the repetitive, high-volume parts of
getting paid:

- **Syncing invoices** from your accounting system (QuickBooks, Xero) continuously.
- **Prioritizing** which overdue invoices to chase first, by likelihood of going
  late and amount at risk — not just by age.
- **Sending reminder sequences** across email and SMS on a sensible cadence:
  pre-due nudge, due-date reminder, escalating follow-ups.
- **Drafting on-brand messages** that match your tone instead of robotic templates.
- **Handling replies** — routing disputes to a human, recognizing "I'll pay
  Friday," and offering payment plans.
- **Reconciling payments** back to the ledger so a paid invoice instantly stops
  getting chased.

## What you should *not* fully automate

Automation earns trust by knowing its limits:

- **Disputes and complaints** → always to a human. An auto-reply to "this invoice
  is wrong" is how you lose a customer.
- **VIP and strategic accounts** → keep an approval step. Some relationships
  warrant a personal touch.
- **Anything near a compliance line** → consent (TCPA/CAN-SPAM/GDPR), quiet hours,
  and opt-outs must be enforced automatically *and* be auditable.

The right mental model is **"approval-gated autonomy"**: the system does the work,
but you decide where it must pause for a human.

## A safe rollout in four steps

1. **Connect and observe.** Sync your AR and let the system score and surface what
   it *would* do before it sends anything. Calibrate.
2. **Turn on early, low-risk touches first.** Pre-due and due-date reminders are
   the highest-ROI, lowest-risk messages. Start there.
3. **Add escalation gradually**, with approval gates on higher-stakes steps and
   VIP accounts.
4. **Measure DSO and recovered cash**, not message volume. The goal is cash in the
   bank, faster — see [how to reduce DSO](/blog/what-is-dso-and-how-to-reduce-it).

## What to look for in a tool

- **Native QuickBooks/Xero sync** with reliable two-way reconciliation.
- **Risk scoring**, so effort goes where it pays off.
- **Tone-safe drafting** with guardrails, not just mail-merge.
- **Built-in consent, suppression, and quiet hours** — compliance can't be an
  afterthought.
- **Auditability** — every send and escalation traceable.
- **No lock-in** — your data and your ledger stay yours.
