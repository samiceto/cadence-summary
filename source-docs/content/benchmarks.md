# DSO Benchmarks by Industry

Use these as **directional ranges**, not targets. DSO is driven by your payment
terms, customer mix, and billing model — a B2B services firm on Net 45 will always
run higher than a retailer paid at point of sale. The fair comparison is your DSO
versus *your own terms* and *your own trend over time*.

## Typical DSO ranges

| Industry | Common terms | Typical DSO range |
|----------|--------------|-------------------|
| Professional & B2B services | Net 30–45 | 40–60 days |
| SaaS / subscription | Net 30 (annual upfront common) | 25–45 days |
| Construction & trades | Net 30–60 (retainage) | 60–90 days |
| Manufacturing / wholesale | Net 30–60 | 45–65 days |
| Staffing & recruiting | Net 30–45 | 45–60 days |
| Healthcare services | Net 30 (payer-dependent) | 40–70 days |
| Marketing & creative agencies | Net 30 | 40–55 days |
| Logistics & freight | Net 30 | 35–50 days |
| Retail / e-commerce (B2B) | Net 15–30 | 20–35 days |

## How to read these

- **At or below the low end of your range** — collections are healthy; protect it.
- **Mid-range** — typical, but usually 5–15 days of improvement is available
  through earlier, more consistent follow-up.
- **Above the high end** — a real cash-flow drag and a bad-debt risk signal. This
  is where disciplined, automated dunning moves the needle fastest.

## What actually moves DSO

Industry sets the baseline; **process** sets where you land within it. The teams
with the lowest DSO for their sector share a few habits:

- They invoice the same day and chase **before** the due date, not just after.
- They prioritize by **risk**, not age alone.
- They make paying a one-click action.
- They're **consistent** — the follow-up happens whether or not anyone has time.

That last point is why automation matters: consistency is hard to sustain by hand.

## Calculate your own DSO

```
DSO = (Accounts Receivable ÷ Total Credit Sales) × Days in Period
```

Track it monthly and watch the trend. If it's drifting up — or sitting above your
industry range — [Cadence](https://cadence.app) can bring it down by automating
the consistent, risk-prioritized follow-up that most teams can't staff for.
